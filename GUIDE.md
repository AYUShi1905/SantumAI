# Santum AI - User Knowledge Ingestion Guide

To get the best results from the AI counselor, the way you "teach" it (ingestion) matters. Follow these tips to ensure the AI has high-quality, clear information.

## 1. Best File Format: DOCX is King 👑
Always prefer **.docx** (Microsoft Word) files over PDFs when possible.
*   **Why?** DOCX files have a logical structure that is much easier for the AI to read.
*   **PDF Risk:** PDFs often include "noise" like page numbers, headers, and footers in the middle of sentences, which can confuse the AI.

## 2. Formatting Tables 📊
If your manual contains tables (e.g., "Technique | Duration | Goal"), ensure they are **simple grids** in Word.
*   Avoid "merged cells" or complex nested tables if possible.
*   The system extracts tables row-by-row, so keep the relationship between columns clear.

## 3. Avoid Scanned Documents 📷
The system **cannot read image-based PDFs** (scanned documents). 
*   If you can't "highlight" or "copy" the text in your PDF, the AI can't read it either.
*   Always use "Searchable" or "Digital" PDFs if you must use the PDF format.

## 4. Chunking & Context 🧠
The AI reads your documents in "chunks" of about 600 tokens (~450 words).
*   **Tip:** Use clear headings (# Heading 1, ## Heading 2) in your Word documents. This helps the system identify where one topic ends and another begins.
*   **Tip:** Keep your sentences concise. Long, run-on sentences can sometimes be split across chunks, making them harder for the AI to understand.

## 5. Cleaning Your Data 🧹
Before uploading a DOCX file:
*   Remove any "Tracked Changes" or "Comments."
*   Delete any unnecessary pages (e.g., Title pages, Table of Contents, Index) to save tokens and keep the "brain" focused only on the actual counseling content.
